My Academic & Personal Interests

Exploring economics, data, and the world around us

Economic Research & Data Analysis

One of my primary passions is economic research and data analysis. In today's data driven world, the ability to extract meaningful insights from large datasets has become increasingly valuable. I've spent considerable time learning statistical programming languages like Python, which have become essential tools in modern economic analysis. Through my coursework and independent projects, I've worked on analyzing various economic datasets, from unemployment trends to consumer spending patterns, using econometric techniques to identify relationships and test hypotheses.


What excites me most about economic research is the detective work involved in formulating questions, gathering evidence, and piecing together narratives that help explain economic phenomena. I recently started a research project examining the impact of health and education on countries around the world, whichs requires collecting data from multiple sources, cleaning and organizing it, and creating visualizations for our analysis. This process is teaching me how to collect and sort data and present it in a clear way to audiences.


Beyond the technical aspects, I'm deeply interested in behavioral economics, the study of psychological, cognitive, and emotional factors that influence economic decisions. The work of economists like Daniel Kahneman and Richard Thaler has shown that people don't always behave as the rational actors assumed in traditional economic models. Understanding these behavioral insights is crucial for designing better policies and business strategies. I'm particularly fascinated by how nudges and choice architecture can be used to help people make better financial decisions without restricting their freedom of choice.


Key Areas of Interest

  • Financial Markets: Analyzing market trends, asset pricing, and investment strategies
  • Econometrics: Applying statistical methods to test economic theories and forecast trends
  • Behavioral Economics: Exploring how psychological factors influence economic decision-making
  • Public Policy: Evaluating the economic impact of government policies and regulations
  • International Trade: Studying global commerce, comparative advantage, and trade agreements
