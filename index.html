About Me

Hello! My name is Henri Pulake, and I am currently an economics student at Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts. I've always been fascinated by how economic principles shape our daily lives, from the decisions individuals make about spending and saving to the policies that governments implement to manage entire economies. My journey into economics began in high school when I first learned about supply and demand curves, and I was immediately hooked by the logical elegance of economic theory combined with its real-world applications.

At Northeastern, I've had the opportunity to dive deep into various areas of economics, including microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, and behavioral economics. One of the aspects I enjoy most about studying economics is its interdisciplinary natureâ€”it draws from mathematics, psychology, political science, and even philosophy. Through my coursework, I've developed strong analytical and quantitative skills, learning to work with statistical software, analyze large datasets, and build economic models that help explain complex phenomena.

Beyond academics, I'm particularly interested in development economics and understanding how economic growth can be achieved in emerging markets. I believe that sound economic policies have the power to lift millions out of poverty and create opportunities for future generations. I'm also passionate about financial literacy and have volunteered to teach basic economic concepts to high school students in the Boston area. After graduation, I hope to work in economic consulting or policy analysis, where I can apply my knowledge to solve real-world problems and contribute to evidence-based decision making.
